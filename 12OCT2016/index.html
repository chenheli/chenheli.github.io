About

The Computing Infrastructure Research Centre (CIRC) has been founded to bring transformative changes in the way data centres and other forms of computing infrastructure are designed, built, and operated. CIRC is the first data centre research facility in Canada and among very few others worldwide, and boasts a pioneering model of university-industry collaboration. Its research is market-focused and needs-driven, ensuring that its resources are fully leveraged in solving problems that create value to industry and society.

Education

CIRC subscribes to the “McMaster Way” of education: experiential learning. It is the only centre in Canada, and among very few others worldwide, that can provide hands-on learning opportunities in the design, operations, and maintenance of data centres (DCs). Presently, CIRC provides excellent research opportunities for graduate students, both at the masters and doctoral level, and postdoctoral fellows. Further, we welcome undergraduate students for short research internships and long term capstone projects. Notable features of CIRC’s education initiatives are:

Innovation Focus
CIRC’s mission in education is to prepare students to participate in the innovation economy. Towards this, all projects and programs are designed with a keen focus on customer discovery, market examination, and product placement

Continuous Industry Engagement
 Through close ties with nearly a dozen industry partners, CIRC offers the opportunity to participate in one of the fastest growing industries in close proximity during the course of study

Training Day
 Once per quarter, CIRC organizes training day, featuring structured hour-long sessions on core scientific concepts and engineering tools, presentations on what’s new, and interactive brainstorming sessions. The sessions are open to the entire McMaster community

Entrepreneurship Training
 All students outstanding entrepreneurial support network of the southern Ontario to foster startup ventures whenever possible

Service

CIRC is the only centre in Canada, and among very few others world-wide, with an industry focused yet vendor-neutral environment for conducting studies concerning the design, operations, and maintenance of data centres. Towards this, CIRC has (1) a reconfigurable data centre, which can be quickly modified into a desired configuration, (2) dedicated laboratories for thermal and electrical testing, and (3) a shop for rapid prototyping of components.

The CIRC team and facilities are available to indsutry patrons for enhancing their business needs, or develop strategies for new opportunities.
